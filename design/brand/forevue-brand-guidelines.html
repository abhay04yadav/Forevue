Brand guidelines · v0.1
01

The idea

Most systems record what happened. Forevue helps you see what's coming.

Every college already owns the data — it's just trapped across eight to twelve systems, and nobody sees the struggling student until the exam hall does. Forevue is the intelligence layer that reads across those systems and turns them into clear, early answers you can act on. It doesn't replace the ERP. It lets you finally see inside it.

Positioning: the intelligence layer for higher education — ask anything in plain English, catch at-risk students early, keep accreditation ready, on one source of truth.

What Forevue is
  • A calm lens over the systems you run
  • Advisory — it surfaces, you decide
  • Grounded — every answer shows its evidence
  • Early — it flags before it's urgent
What Forevue is not
  • Not another ERP or rip-and-replace
  • Not an autopilot that acts for you
  • Not a black box — it never guesses
  • Not alarmist about students

Four traits — and what they rule out.

Clear

Grounded

Respectful

Because Forevue handles minors' data and tells institutions which students are struggling, the brand has to feel trustworthy and humane — which rules out the "disrupt your campus with AI" energy most edtech reaches for.

06

Voice & messaging

Sound the way the product behaves.

Plain verbs, sentence case. Say what happens, simply.
Lead with the specific. "Six students in CSE-3," not "actionable insights."
Put the evidence on the table. Every claim names what it's based on.
Dignity for every student. Never alarmist, never a label without care.
Confident, never boastful. No 🚀, no "revolutionary," no hype.
In voice
"Six students in CSE-3 slipped below the attendance line this week. Here's why, and who to talk to."
Off voice
"🚀 Our AI-powered platform empowers institutions to leverage data-driven insights!"

Say this, not that

Forevue surfaces / shows / flags  —  not "decides," "acts," "fixes"
students who need attention  —  not "failures," "problem students"
the systems you already run  —  not "rip and replace your ERP"
with the evidence behind it  —  not unexplained scores
consent-gated · minor — consent required  —  never share a minor's data quietly

07

Signature device & imagery

The resolve, everywhere.

The convergence — scattered points becoming one clear forward line — is the single thing Forevue is remembered by. Use it as the deck's opening moment, the website hero, the app splash, and loading states. One memorable element; keep everything around it quiet.

Imagery: real Indian campus texture and evidence/citation motifs — never the glowing-blue-AI-brain stock. Iconography: simple line icons, rounded caps, single weight, teal or ink. Deliberately avoid gradient-mesh purple, node-network logos, and "empower with AI" clichés.

09

Brand layer ↔ product layer

Two layers, one spine.

The brand (deck, site, marketing) carries warmth — Spectral, amber, paper. The product UI stays calm and functional — Public Sans and the existing design-system tokens (the cool neutrals, the colour-vision-safe risk tiers). The shared teal #0E7C86 is the through-line that makes them feel like one company. When in doubt inside the app, defer to the product design system, not these marketing styles.

Governance shows up in the brand, not just the code: every AI answer is shown with its evidence (grounded-or-abstain), all generated content is a human-owned draft (advisory, never auto-finalized), and minors are always marked consent-required (DPDP). The brand should never imply Forevue acts on its own.
